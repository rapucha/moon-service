Moon Service

Moon Service helps photographers spot upcoming windows when the Moon is low, ambient light is useful, and the forecast is worth checking before heading out.

Search opportunities

What It Is

An anonymous city lookup for Moon photography opportunities. Enter a city or town to see ranked windows with Moon position, light, weather, score, and caveats in one place.

Why It Exists

Planning a Moon photo usually means checking ephemeris data, twilight, weather, and timing separately. Moon Service combines those signals into a quick first pass so you can decide whether a night or morning is worth scouting.

Current Boundaries

  • No account, email, cookies, or saved server-side user profile is required.
  • City or town searches are enough; exact home addresses are not needed.
  • The backend sends the location text you submit to Open-Meteo for lookup. For weather forecasts, it sends the resolved location's coordinates rounded to four decimal places.
  • Open-Meteo says free-API logs may contain sensitive geographic request information and are deleted after 90 days. See its Terms & Privacy.
  • Forecasts and scores are decision support, not a guarantee of clear skies or a usable horizon.
  • Local hills, buildings, trees, and other horizon obstructions are not modelled yet.

Data and Imagery

Data sources: Weather data comes from Open-Meteo (CC BY 4.0). Location data comes from Open-Meteo Geocoding, is based on GeoNames, and is treated as CC BY-NC 4.0 for this noncommercial alpha. Moon Service adapts and aggregates these data and applies its own scoring.
